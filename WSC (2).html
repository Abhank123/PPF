The World Scholar's Cup
The World Scholar's Cup (WSC) is an international academic tournament, comprising of four main events namely: Debate, Collaborative Writing, the Scholar's Challenge (based on the WSC Syllabus), and the Scholar's Bowl (also based on the WSC Syllabus). More than that, however, the World Scholar's Cup is a place to celebrate learning, and culture.
Debating in the World Scholar's Cup is truly an energizing experience. A few pointers are listed below:

1. There are three rounds in which teams can either be positive (for the motion) or negative (against the motion).
2. In each round, a team will get fifteen minutes to research and discuss with each other.
3. The debate will then begin with the positive side going first.
4. After all speakers have spoken, there is then a feedback round.
5. The winner is announced.
